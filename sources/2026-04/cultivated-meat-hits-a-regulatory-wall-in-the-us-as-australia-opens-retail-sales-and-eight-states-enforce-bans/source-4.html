Cultured Meat Market Forecast to 2031: North America Holds Largest Share as Market Reaches USD 114.51 Million, Says Mordor Intelligence

The global cultured meat market is set for rapid expansion, growing at a CAGR of 21.48% by 2031.Driven by advancements in cell-cultivation technologies and cost-efficient hybrid formulations, the market is gaining strong momentum across foodservice and early commercialization channels.

April 01, 2026 10:38 ET  | Source: Mordor Intelligence Mordor Intelligence

Hyderabad, April 01, 2026 (GLOBE NEWSWIRE) -- According to a recent study by Mordor Intelligence, the cultured meat market size was valued at USD 35.55 million in 2025 and is projected to grow from USD 43.27 million in 2026 to USD 114.51 million by 2031, registering a CAGR of 21.48% during the forecast period. The market is gaining momentum due to advancements in cell-cultivation technologies, increasing regulatory approvals, and a strategic shift toward cost-efficient hybrid formulations. As sustainability and food security concerns intensify, the cultured meat market is emerging as a transformative segment within the global protein industry. 

Regional Outlook Highlighting Growth Opportunities 

North America leads the global cultured meat market, supported by regulatory approvals and strong innovation ecosystems. The United States remains a key market, although regional regulatory variations create challenges for nationwide expansion. 

Asia-Pacific is the fastest-growing region, driven by supportive regulatory frameworks and increasing investments in food innovation. Singapore, in particular, has emerged as a global hub for cultivated meat commercialization due to its streamlined approval processes. 

Europe presents a mixed landscape, with countries like the Netherlands supporting innovation through funding, while others impose regulatory restrictions. Emerging markets remain largely untapped due to the absence of clear regulatory frameworks. 

Cultured Meat Market Trends Shaping Growth 

Hybrid Formulations Reducing Cost Barriers 

Hybrid products are playing a crucial role in driving cultured meat market growth by lowering the cost-to-market barriers associated with cell-based production. By blending cultivated cells with plant-based matrices, manufacturers can significantly reduce reliance on expensive bioreactor output. This strategy not only improves affordability but also enhances product texture and scalability. The cultured meat market analysis indicates that hybrid formulations will dominate early commercialization phases until pure-cell production achieves cost parity with conventional meat. 

Foodservice Partnerships Accelerating Market Adoption 

Food service channels are emerging as a key entry point for the cultured meat market, enabling companies to test products and gather consumer feedback before expanding into retail. Collaborations with restaurants and chefs help introduce cultivated meat in familiar formats, reducing consumer hesitation and improving acceptance. These partnerships also allow brands to refine taste, texture, and pricing strategies, supporting faster market validation and adoption. 

Poultry Segment Driving Early Commercialization 

Poultry is leading the cultured meat market share, primarily due to its biological advantages in cell growth and production efficiency. Faster cell proliferation cycles and lower resource requirements make poultry-based products more cost-effective compared to beef and other red meats. This advantage is enabling quicker commercialization and scaling, positioning poultry as the dominant segment in the early stages of the cultured meat market forecast. However, advancements in bovine and seafood cell lines are expected to diversify the market in the long term. 

Shift Toward Ground and Processed Formats 

Producers are focusing on ground and processed formats such as burgers, nuggets, and sausages to accelerate cultured meat market growth. These formats are easier to produce and do not require complex tissue engineering, making them suitable for early-stage commercialization. Ground products also align with consumer expectations, as they prioritize taste and convenience over structural authenticity. This strategic shift is enabling companies to generate revenue while continuing to develop whole-cut products.

Competitive Landscape and Industry Strategy 

The cultured meat market is moderately fragmented, with companies competing across product types, species, and technologies. Leading players are focusing on hybrid formulations, regulatory approvals, and strategic partnerships to gain early market share. 

Cost reduction remains a key priority, with companies investing in vertical integration and alternative growth media to improve production efficiency. Asset-light business models and collaborations with foodservice providers are also gaining traction as firms navigate funding constraints.

Global Red Meat Market Analysis - The global red meat market, valued at USD 0.86 trillion in 2025, is projected to reach USD 1.01 trillion by 2031, growing at a CAGR of 2.62%. This steady expansion is primarily fueled by shifting protein demands in emerging nations and significant investments in automated slaughter technology and cold-chain infrastructure.  

Pork Meat Market Size - The global pork meat market stood at USD 406.76 billion in 2025 and is estimated to reach USD 466.13 billion by 2031, with a CAGR of 2.30%. Consumption is increasingly driven by fitness enthusiasts and athletes who value pork for its high protein and essential vitamin content, alongside a surge in global tourism and on-the-go food consumption.  

Deli Meat Market Share - The deli meat market is experiencing a notable surge, valued at USD 207.86 billion in 2025 and expected to reach USD 271.61 billion by 2031 at a CAGR of 4.56%. This growth is heavily influenced by younger consumers who view deli meats as a premium snacking option, further amplified by the social media popularity of charcuterie boards.  

Halal Meat Market Size - The halal meat market represents one of the most dynamic segments in the protein industry, valued at USD 1.06 trillion in 2025 and forecast to reach USD 1.52 trillion by 2031 at a robust CAGR of 6.88%. Demand is surging not only among the expanding Muslim population but also among non-Muslim consumers who associate halal certification with higher safety and hygiene standards. While poultry remains the most affordable and dominant meat type, the rise of halal tourism and standardized certification across Europe and Asia-Pacific is providing new avenues for high-value export growth.
