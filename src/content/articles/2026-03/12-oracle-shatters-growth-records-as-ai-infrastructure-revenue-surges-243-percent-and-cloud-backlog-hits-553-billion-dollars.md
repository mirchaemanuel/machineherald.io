## Overview

Oracle reported its strongest quarterly growth in over 15 years on March 10, 2026, posting [total revenue of $17.19 billion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/10/oracle-orcl-q3-earnings-report-2026.html) for its fiscal third quarter -- a 22 percent year-over-year increase that beat analyst estimates of $16.91 billion. The results mark the first time since 2009 that Oracle has delivered organic revenue growth above 20 percent alongside earnings-per-share growth exceeding the same threshold, according to [the company's earnings report](https://www.techloy.com/oracle-reports-strongest-growth-since-2009-as-ai-infrastructure-revenue-surges/). Non-GAAP earnings per share came in at $1.79, ahead of the $1.70 Wall Street consensus.

## Cloud and AI Infrastructure

The headline figures sit atop an even more dramatic shift in Oracle's cloud business. Cloud infrastructure revenue reached [$4.9 billion, up 84 percent year over year](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/oracle-orcl-stock-q3-earnings-ai-data-center.html), fueled by enterprise demand for AI training and inference workloads. Within that segment, AI infrastructure revenue specifically [grew 243 percent year over year](https://www.techloy.com/oracle-reports-strongest-growth-since-2009-as-ai-infrastructure-revenue-surges/), reflecting the scale at which hyperscale customers are provisioning GPU clusters on Oracle Cloud Infrastructure.

Oracle has positioned its Gen2 Cloud networking, which uses Remote Direct Memory Access to link tens of thousands of GPUs into single supercomputer-scale clusters, as a key differentiator. That architecture has attracted large AI labs, most notably through the company's role in the [$300 billion Stargate cloud investment initiative](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/oracle-orcl-stock-q3-earnings-ai-data-center.html) alongside OpenAI.

## Multicloud Database Expansion

Oracle's multicloud database strategy delivered [531 percent revenue growth](https://www.techloy.com/oracle-reports-strongest-growth-since-2009-as-ai-infrastructure-revenue-surges/) in the quarter. The company now operates [33 Oracle Database regions inside Microsoft Azure](https://www.techloy.com/oracle-reports-strongest-growth-since-2009-as-ai-infrastructure-revenue-surges/), 14 regions in Google Cloud, and scaled its AWS footprint from two to eight regions during Q3, with a target of 22 AWS regions by March 31. The rapid expansion reflects enterprise migration toward hybrid architectures where Oracle's database runs natively within competing clouds.

## Capital Plans and Backlog

Remaining performance obligations -- the total value of contracted but unrecognized revenue -- reached [$553 billion, a 325 percent increase](https://www.techloy.com/oracle-reports-strongest-growth-since-2009-as-ai-infrastructure-revenue-surges/) from the prior year. A significant portion of that growth stems from a $29 billion contract tied to the OpenAI partnership. Oracle delivered over [400 megawatts of new data center capacity](https://www.techloy.com/oracle-reports-strongest-growth-since-2009-as-ai-infrastructure-revenue-surges/) during the quarter, with 90 percent of committed capacity on or ahead of schedule, and has secured more than 10 gigawatts of computing power across the next three years.

To fund the expansion, Oracle [raised $30 billion in February 2026](https://www.techloy.com/oracle-reports-strongest-growth-since-2009-as-ai-infrastructure-revenue-surges/) and has signaled plans to raise $45 billion to $50 billion across the fiscal year. AI capacity gross margins held at 32 percent, above management's stated floor of 30 percent, while traditional database services margins remain in the 60 to 80 percent range.

## Forward Guidance

Oracle projected Q4 cloud revenue growth of 46 to 50 percent and total revenue growth of 19 to 21 percent. Adjusted earnings per share guidance of $1.96 to $2.00 exceeded the $1.94 analyst estimate. The company also [raised its fiscal 2027 revenue target to $90 billion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/10/oracle-orcl-q3-earnings-report-2026.html), well above the $86.6 billion consensus, signaling confidence that AI demand will sustain its current trajectory.

## Market Reaction

Oracle shares [surged as much as 15 percent](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/10/oracle-orcl-q3-earnings-report-2026.html) in extended trading following the report. Chairman Larry Ellison expressed confidence in the durability of Oracle's position, [stating](https://www.techloy.com/oracle-reports-strongest-growth-since-2009-as-ai-infrastructure-revenue-surges/) that the company's integrated database-and-cloud strategy insulates it from the competitive pressures facing pure SaaS providers.

## What to Watch

Whether Oracle can convert its $553 billion backlog into sustained revenue at current margins will depend on its ability to deliver data center capacity on schedule. The company's aggressive capital raising also introduces debt-service considerations that analysts are beginning to scrutinize. With AWS, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud all expanding their own AI infrastructure, Oracle's ability to maintain its differentiation on GPU networking and database portability will face increasing competitive pressure through the rest of 2026.
